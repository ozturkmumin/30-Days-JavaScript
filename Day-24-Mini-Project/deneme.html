What is Forex?

The forex market is the financial market where traders exchange currencies to make a profit from price changes. It is the largest and most liquid market in the world with an average daily trading volume of $5 trillion. Traders are able to trade currencies from anywhere with an Internet connection and at any time as long as the market is open and they can buy and sell currencies with the aim of making a profit.

What are the Forex trading hours?

Traders can trade 24 hours and 5 days a week. The forex market is open 24 hours a day, from Sunday evening until Friday night. Since there are various international time zones overlapping, trading is possible any time of the day or night on weekdays. There are major trading sessions in four locations:

What is traded in the forex market?

In the forex market, you trade currency pairs based on the forecast of currency exchange rates. Forex traders buy or sell currency pairs and speculate on the price difference to benefit from this difference. One of the most widely traded forex pairs is the euro and US dollar.

What is Spread in Forex Trading?

The spread in forex trading is the difference between the buying/bid price and the selling/ask price of a currency pair. In the forex market, a ‘buy’ price is often above the market price, and a ‘sell’ price is often below the market price. This is the difference between the bid and the asking price that shows the current and actual spread in the forex market and the additional spread added by the forex broker. Lower (tighter) spreads are preferred by the traders because it makes the trade less expensive. It also shows that the currency pair is highly liquid and not so volatile, which makes the trade less risky. If the market is very volatile and not very liquid, it is possible to see wide (high) spreads. What is Leverage in Forex Trading?

When trading, forex leverage allows traders to control a larger position with less of their own funds. It allows traders to make more volume transactions by money requirement which is usually ‘borrowed’ from the forex broker. Traders can usually get more leverage on forex than other financial instruments. So, they can control a larger sum of money with a smaller deposit.

Here is an example to understand the leverage procedure: Imagine that you have a 1.000 USD deposit and want to invest worth 100.000 USD. In this scenario, your leverage ratio is 1:100, which means you control 100 times higher than your main deposit. A 1% rise means a 1.000 USD gain while a 1% drop means there is nothing left. So, a forex trader should always consider the risk associated with leverage.
